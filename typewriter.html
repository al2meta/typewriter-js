The Sleepy park
By Anthony L. Yap



I dream of a park in my early days
Lighted by dim lights from some lonely posts
A deafening silence rules the place
An eerie atmosphere surrounds the air


In that slumber for hours maybe
I stroll the park through darkness thin
A feeling of melancholia embraces me
To my heart's content it pleases me


A solemn place for my solitude
Not a lively soul confined with me
I walk alone no company
I traverse that park for eternity
